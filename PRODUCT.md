# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Potential photography clients across three verticals: corporate event organizers (companies, schools, galas) who prioritize reliability and professional delivery; sports organizations, clubs, and event promoters who need fast turnaround and proven action competence; and commercial brands or agencies seeking a creative partner for campaigns and brand content. All three book based on trust, professionalism, and evidence of craft — not on charm or hype. They arrive at this site having already searched, so the job is confirmation, not persuasion.

## Product Purpose

A single-photographer portfolio and enquiry site for Julien Mann, Montréal-based photographer specializing in sport, events, people, and commercial work. The site exists to establish professional credibility, present work across service verticals, and convert visits into enquiries. Success is a filled contact form. There are no bookings, no galleries beyond examples, no commerce — just a clean path from "who is this person?" to "I'd like to work with them."

## Brand Personality

Editorial precision meets direct, unembellished honesty. The voice is confident without posturing: "good photos, no fuss," "just me, a camera, and your brief." Three words: precise, candid, direct. The emotional register is professional warmth — not cold and corporate, not folksy and approachable — more like a skilled tradesperson who takes the craft seriously but doesn't make the job about themselves. Bilingual EN/FR — Montréal is the base.

## Anti-references

- **Soft stock-photo photography sites** — script fonts, pastel tones, "Capturing your precious moments," saccharine warmth. Forbidden.
- **SaaS/startup aesthetic** — Inter or Geist, blue CTAs, hero metric cards, white backgrounds with purple gradients, rounded-everything. This is a photography portfolio, not a product.
- **Dark neon / cyberpunk** — purple or cyan glows, glassmorphism, neon-on-black. Wrong register entirely.
- **Overwrought agency portfolios** — 10-second loading intros, every element animated independently, cursor that leaves sparkles. The design detail should feel intentional, not theatrical.

## Design Principles

1. **Craft earns the right to be seen.** The visual language should be as precise as the photography it represents. Every spacing decision, every typographic choice is a signal of the same eye that frames a shot.
2. **Direct means direct.** No header that restates the nav item. No intro paragraph that explains what a photographer does. The site talks like the copy: get to the point, then get out of the way.
3. **The work is the argument.** The layout exists to surface photographs and make enquiries easy — not to demonstrate web design skills. No element should compete with the images.
4. **Dark by default, for a reason.** Photographs read better on a dark field. The choice is functional, not aesthetic fashion.
5. **Bilingual without compromise.** EN and FR carry equal weight. Neither is a fallback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Custom cursor replaced with default on touch/no-hover devices (already implemented). Respect prefers-reduced-motion for all entrance animations. Bilingual site currently covers EN/FR — both languages are fully translated throughout.
